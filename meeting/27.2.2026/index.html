Apertus invites you

We are excited to invite you to the founding meeting of the Public AI Switzerland Cooperative. This cooperative aims to bring together individuals and organizations committed to the responsible development and deployment of public AI systems, with a focus on the Swiss context. As a grassroots counterpart of the Swiss AI Initiative, Public AI CH will play a critical role in advancing open, transparent, and publicly beneficial AI technologies.

Why Join the Cooperative?

  • Be part of a pioneering initiative to democratize AI access in Switzerland
  • Collaborate with a diverse group of experts, businesses, and institutions
  • Support the development of locally adapted, open-source AI solutions
  • Contribute to shaping the future of AI in Switzerland, aligned with Swiss values

Key Benefits for Members:

  • Access to cutting-edge AI tools and resources developed in an open source community
  • Opportunities for collaboration on AI projects with the Swiss AI Initiative and network
  • Potential for co-ownership and decision-making within the cooperative
  • Influence in the cooperative's strategic direction

We use Apertus all the time, including in the creation of this document.


About Public AI

The Public AI Inference Utility is a nonprofit, open-source project. Our team builds products and organizes advocacy to support the work of public AI model builders like the Swiss AI Initiative, AI Singapore, AI Sweden, and the Barcelona Supercomputing Center. We believe in public AI—AI as public infrastructure like highways, water, or electricity. Think of a Migros for AI, a BBC for AI, a public utility for AI, or public libraries for AI.

What we do

The Swiss AI Initiative (SNAI) is a collaborative effort between ETH Zurich, EPFL, CSCS, with contributions from partners from industry and universities to develop AI solutions like Apertus: a pioneering research effort prioritizing transparency, openness, and public benefit. The Public AI Switzerland cooperative is not yet a member, but has been supportive of this initiative: ensuring grassroots support that is grounded in principles of global cooperation. We were the first to launch an online chat powered by Apertus 70B, on the day that it became available.

Who are we

You may have heard us writing and discussing these topics online. You may have seen us at a hackathon. We are a diverse and independent group of thinkers, coders and doers who share a commitment to help prepare our communities, and the world, for AI. We don’t just talk about transparency: we act on it. The full membership list will be published after the founding meeting.

The Problem

Switzerland increasingly relies on foreign AI platforms offering global, one-size-fits-all services. These platforms are powerful for many use cases, but they are governed from Silicon Valley or Shenzhen and optimized for global scale rather than local norms, industries, and communities. At the same time, our own open model—Apertus—exists, but requires reliable hosting, tooling, and especially distribution to be usable in practice.

The Solution

Public AI Switzerland makes Swiss-made AI affordable and easy to use. We aggregate demand for Apertus and other open models through a nation-wide cooperative; instead of paying CHF 240 a year for ChatGPT, members pay CHF 100 to own a piece of their own AI. Heavier API users pay transparent overages priced below commercial APIs, and any surplus is reinvested into capacity, lower prices, or community programs. And our distribution network is designed to extend beyond inference, so that other Swiss-made technologies can reach users without rebuilding infrastructure or distribution from scratch.

We are already the primary global deployer of Apertus, operating servers, APIs, chat interfaces, and integrations. Since Apertus launched, more than 35,000 users have registered on our platform, with trillions of tokens served in just a few months—demonstrating real demand for a stable, Swiss-run alternative that developers and institutions can build on over time.

Why Now

Switzerland has a rich tradition of cooperative enterprises (Migros, Coop, Raiffeisen, Mobility). These models emphasize member ownership, democratic governance, and reinvestment of profits for member benefit. They have repeatedly proven effective at providing essential infrastructure at scale while maintaining public trust and price discipline.

AI has now become comparable critical infrastructure, but control is consolidating around a small number of foreign platforms with opaque governance and rising prices. Switzerland already has the core ingredients for an alternative: a sovereign model (Apertus), strong data protection law, and deep trust in cooperative institutions.

The remaining bottleneck is no longer technical. It is distribution, governance, and trust. That gap is now narrow enough to close—but only if done before proprietary platforms become entrenched.

Why create a Swiss AI cooperative?

Switzerland increasingly relies on foreign AI platforms governed from Silicon Valley or Shenzhen, optimized for global scale rather than local norms, industries, and communities. These platforms have opaque governance and rising prices. It's time for a new model—one we collectively own and control, built on Switzerland's rich tradition of cooperative enterprises.

What's a co-op?

A cooperative, or co-op, is a business owned and democratically controlled by its members. Public AI Switzerland is owned by its membership base of developers, institutions, supporters, and workers. Think Migros, Coop, Raiffeisen, or Mobility—the same proven model applied to AI infrastructure.

How does this compare to ChatGPT/OpenAI?

Instead of paying CHF 240 a year for ChatGPT, members pay CHF 100 to own a piece of their own AI. Heavier API users pay transparent overages priced below commercial APIs. All data stays in Switzerland, and any surplus is reinvested into capacity, lower prices, or community programs—not shareholder profits.

What are the membership fees and what do I get?

Supporters & Allies: CHF 100 per year for Founding Supporter Membership. Developers & Institutions: FREE Founding Membership. All members receive co-ownership, governance rights, access to the platform (API + Chat UI), Founding Member status, membership certificate, and ability to influence platform policies. Supporters get access to Apertus 70B chatbot (8B remains free for all).

Is there financial liability?

No, there is no personal financial liability associated with being a co-op member of Public AI Switzerland. Your liability is limited to your membership contribution.

What personal information do I need to provide?

We require minimal information: your name, a valid email address, and your mailing address. This is necessary for maintaining membership records and communications.

Will joining affect my use of other AI platforms?

Not at all. Joining Public AI Switzerland doesn't limit your ability to use other platforms.

Is this a crypto/blockchain thing?

No. Public AI Switzerland is a traditional cooperative enterprise, following the proven Swiss cooperative model.

When will the platform be ready?

The platform is already live! We are the primary global deployer of Apertus, with 35,000+ users and trillions of tokens served. You can try it today at chat.publicai.co or access the API at platform.publicai.co/docs.

Who can join?

Public AI Switzerland is open to Swiss residents and companies. We welcome developers, institutions, researchers, and supporters who want to collectively own and shape Swiss AI infrastructure.

How is data handled?

All data is Swiss-hosted and stays within Swiss boundaries. Data sent via API stays in Switzerland, and all chat history and inputs reside in Switzerland. We comply with Swiss data protection law.

What models are available?

We primarily deploy Apertus, the open and reproducible LLM developed by the Swiss AI Initiative. We also support other open models. Check our API documentation for current model availability.

Can I use this for commercial purposes?

Yes! Many businesses and institutions are already using our platform for commercial purposes. The API is designed for building custom software and automation, and the Chat UI is suitable for organizational use.
